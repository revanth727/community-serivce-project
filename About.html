History of Horticulture

The first known uses of horticulture date back to the ancient world, specifically in Egypt. As humans transitioned from a nomadic, hunting and gathering lifestyle to a semi-sedentary lifestyle, they began to build settlements in regions where water supplies were plentiful. Water is essential to human life just as it is to plant life. Historians suggest that ancient Egyptians borrowed techniques from other human settlements in the Middle East, but they cultivated the largest variety of plants by far. Over 200 species of plants were grown for consumption and appearance in ancient Egypt. Horticulture is the science of cultivating plants for consumption or ornamental purposes. Landscaping is one of the leading purposes for horticulture practices.

Types of horticulture

Horticulture may be a single branch of agriculture, but it contains many practicesand types of farming. The types
of horticulture vary from crops produced for consumption to those grown solely for their appearance. The major
types of horticulture include:


1.Olericulture
2.Pomology
3.Viticulture
4.Floriculture
5.Turf Management
6.Arboriculture
7.Landscape Horticulture
8.Postharvest Physiology
9.Oenology


Each of these divisions of horticulture will be explored in the following sections. They are equally important and
continue to be researched and developed to better the practice of agriculture as a whole.

Scope of horticulture

> Horticulture crops produce a higher yield per hectare than field crops.

>Horticulture crops are extremely valuable because of their high nutritional value. Fruits and vegetables,
 in particular, provide us with a lot of vitamins and minerals.

>Horticulture is very important because it improves the beauty of the environment.

>Small and marginal farmers can benefit from horticulture crops.
